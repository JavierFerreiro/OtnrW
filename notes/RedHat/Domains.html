What is a Domain?

For describe what is a domain, I think that the best approach is explain the the reason for what someone invented it.

In a nutshell, the cause of the dns is comfort :) (Yeah! lazy power!). The computers insist on use the numbers for everything and it's no exception the directions of the computers in the Internet. This directions are writed in numbers,i.e on ipv4 192.168.0.10. Yes, it was more easy for humans some directions like: the computer in United States, Los Angeles California,Syscamorre Avenue number whatever. But this, it's not really funtional for computers.

Then what you need for contact the computer that host the web page of google for do a search, you can tell to your browser: http://173.194.78.100 and your browser can search this direction and present the web page or... you can use dns.

The idea behind dns is that the directions of computers are no easy to memorize for persons, who are get acostumated to remember names. Then, Paul V.Mockapetris design a translator, computer->human, human->computer, the dns service.

when you write some direction in your browser, i.e. http://google.com, the first thing that the computer do is translate google.com (a domain) to numbers 173.194.78.10 (or whatever). Thanks to this service you don't need to remember a lot of large numbers, you just remember the name of the pages.

How is organized the domains

The domains are organized in an inverted tree form, aka as domain namespace. In this tree the root node is represented by ".". In the level under the root we find the top level domain (TLD): com (for commercial),edu (educational),it (italy)... (look familiar,isn't?)

Inside of this last leaf, we can obtain a name, for example, google buy his name and has google.com. After it google can construct another sub-domains like drive.google.com (host drive in the domain google). Of this form it's constructed the inverted tree.
